WaterSoftHack — Two Year Impact Summary

Transforming water science through cyberinfrastructure, machine learning, and training

Over its first two years, WaterSoftHack has emerged as a transformative training and innovation initiative, advancing the integration of cyberinfrastructure and machine learning into water science and engineering. Through hands-on training, software development, and career-building opportunities, the program has significantly strengthened research capacity, fostered collaborations, and accelerated the adoption of data-driven solutions for water challenges worldwide.

Key Impacts

  • Capacity Building: Trained over 350 water science researchers, students, engineers, and industry professionals, equipping them with cutting-edge skills in cyberinfrastructure, machine learning, and data-driven water resources modeling.
  • Fellowship Development: Mentored and trained 27 WaterSoftHack Fellows (13 women), preparing them as the next generation of leaders in AI, cyberinfrastructure, and water science & engineering research. Fellows engaged in hands-on projects, collaborative hackathons, and interdisciplinary Capstone projects.
  • Software Innovation: Designed and released the WaterSoft package — a comprehensive, open-source toolkit enabling scalable and efficient machine learning applications in water science and engineering. View the WaterSoft repo.
  • Career Advancement: Celebrated major career milestones, including two female Fellows securing tenure-track faculty positions at U.S. universities — demonstrating the program’s impact on professional growth and leadership in the field.
  • Community Engagement: Built an active network of researchers and professionals, fostering collaborations between universities, government agencies, and private sector partners to tackle urgent water-related challenges.
  • Sustainable Impact: Created a foundation for long-term collaboration and technology transfer, empowering participants to integrate machine learning and cyberinfrastructure solutions into water science and engineering research and education.
